About Me

When I’m asked why I chose to major in computer science, I don’t have a single answer. CS is a vast field and there are so many directions that I can go in with the skills I’ve gained learning to code. I pursued computer science in college despite having no prior experience because I wanted to challenge myself to learn something unfamiliar. Learning to code has had a strong impact on my life, equipping me to solve tough problems using a versatile skillset. I continually challenge myself to learn more and explore what I don’t know.

Outside of CS, I spend my time doing volunteer work, such as my position as operations coordinator at Camp Kesem at University of Washington, and working on music. These experiences have shaped me into a responsible, disciplined, and thoughtful leader.
